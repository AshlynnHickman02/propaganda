Statistics

Here are the statistics of mathematics in highschool, including passing rates, failing rates, and how much time the subject wastes.

Passing Rates

Before fall break, students were required to take Benchmarks for the Math and English subjects. The math benchmark was called the ACT Benchmarks, and it's shocking to see how many students passed even a 4th grade question. Only 31.77% of students who took the ACT Benchmarks at Valley Vista Highschool passed a 4th grade question. Additionally, only 10.54% of Valley Vista students passed a 5th grade question.

Failing Rates

Only 50% of students pass with a C and above. That's quite a gawking number of students not being able to graduate because they failed their math class.

The Amount of Time Math Wastes

Highschool students are very busy trying to juggle each class, clubs, work, and sports. 3.3 million students attending Highschool in the United States have a job. That's at least 5 hours off a weekday. 7.9 million students are in sports or clubs, taking at least 2 hours off of their time daily. 1.4 million student in the U.S. are taking AP classes, which after school can take at least an hour or two to complete the work for that class, considering it is a college elective. Math homework in highschool can take from 90 minutes to 2 hours. So, altogether, that leaves the student 11 hours after school. If school would end everywhere at 2:15, the student would go to sleep at 9pm, no including chores at home, other classes' homework and projects, and self care. That's a lot of time put to waste.
